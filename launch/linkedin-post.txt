I stopped treating AI like a chat box.

The problem was never the model.

The problem was memory.

Every serious project has a private mess behind it:

- strategy notes
- old decisions
- research
- PDFs
- legal docs
- credentials
- things you cannot paste into ChatGPT

So every session starts the same way:

"Here is the project."
"Here is the folder."
"Here is what we tried."
"Here is the decision from last week."
"Here is the thing you forgot yesterday."

That is not agentic work.

That is manual onboarding.

So I built fbrain.

Plain English:

A locked memory folder for AI tools.

Claude Code, Codex, Cursor, and OpenCode can search the right project background without dumping the whole project into chat.

Private files stay locked.
Sensitive context stays separated.
Secret checks run before GitHub.

The shift is subtle but huge:

I do not give AI everything.

I give it the right memory.

Bigger prompts were the wrong answer.

Safer context is the unlock.
